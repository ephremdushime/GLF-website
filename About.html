Who We Are: The Girls Leaders Forum

Empowering university students to mentor and lead the next generation of youth in Rwanda.

Our Mission

To cultivate a community of young Rwandan leaders equipped with essential knowledge in sexual and reproductive health, entrepreneurship, and digital literacy. We aim to dismantle barriers to youth success through peer-to-peer mentorship and community engagement.

Our Vision

A Rwanda where every young person, regardless of background, has the confidence, resources, and support to achieve their full potential, contributing to a vibrant, safe, and equitable society.

Our History and Impact

Founded by dedicated students at the University of Rwanda, the Girls Leaders Forum began as a small study circle aimed at supporting female peers. Over the years, we recognized the deeper need for grassroots intervention in key areas affecting youth development.

Since our inception, we have directly mentored over 500 students and run awareness campaigns reaching thousands of parents and community members, culminating in the "Parental Voices for a Safe Future" initiative.

Core Values:

  • Integrity & Trust
  • Collaboration
  • Sustainable Impact

Our Structure: Led by Students, Driven by Community

GLF is managed by a volunteer-based executive committee of students from the University of Rwanda, ensuring a fresh, relevant approach to youth issues.
