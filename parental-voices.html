Campaign Launch | October 1, 2025

GLF Launches 'Parental Voices for a Safe Future'

Empowering Rwandan parents as the first educators to tackle adolescent sexual and reproductive health challenges head-on.

By Girls Leaders Forum (GLF), University of Rwanda

1. Background

Rwanda continues to face challenges in adolescent sexual and reproductive health (SRH). In 2021, over 20,000 teenage pregnancies were reported, alongside increasing cases of STIs and sexual harassment.

While schools and health facilities provide SRH education, many adolescents lack culturally appropriate guidance at home. Silence and stigma around sexuality leave young people vulnerable to misinformation.

The 'Parental Voices for Safe Future' campaign seeks to address this by empowering parents as the first educators, building their capacity for open, value-based conversations with their children.

2. Key Objectives

  • Raise awareness on the importance of parental involvement in adolescent SRH.
  • Build parents’ capacity to deliver accurate, age-appropriate, and culturally relevant information.
  • Foster trust and dialogue between parents and children.
  • Contribute to the reduction of teenage pregnancies, STIs, and sexual harassment.

3. Campaign Approach (8 Weeks)

This will be an 8-week intensive campaign, focusing on engagement and capacity building across multiple community levels.

Key Activities:

  • Stakeholder mobilization (including universities, government, NGOs, and religious leaders).
  • Training youth facilitators (GLF members) in SRH communication.
  • Organizing Parent–child dialogue forums in targeted communities.
  • Conducting school-based workshops with parents, teachers, and learners.
  • Religious engagement to amplify messages.
  • Media outreach via radio, posters, social media, and theatre.
  • Final forum to share outcomes, testimonies, and recommendations.

Target Groups:

The primary beneficiaries are Parents and guardians of adolescents (10–19 years). Secondary beneficiaries include adolescents, teachers, religious leaders, local authorities, and community health workers.

The campaign will cover both urban and rural communities, focusing on areas with high teenage pregnancy rates.

4. Partnership Opportunities

We invite NGOs, development partners, and private companies to support this youth-led initiative. The total estimated budget for the 8-week campaign is RWF 3,500,000.

Support can be provided through technical expertise, financial contributions, or in-kind support (e.g., printing, airtime, venues, refreshments). Your partnership will amplify impact, visibility, and sustainability.
